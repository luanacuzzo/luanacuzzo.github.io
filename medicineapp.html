Purelife App - A Telehealth Platform

Project Details

Design Challenge | 2 weeks | Figma

UX Strategy | UX Writing | Interface Audit | UI Redesign

Personal Goal

To challenge myself to solve a complex problem in a short time frame, and with limited resources through the Lean UX Methodology.

DISCOVERY

The Problem

Don't you agree that it is just crazy that people go to the hospital to get help on a disease or condition, but they fear they might get sicker? Doctor's offices are indeed vectors of illnessess. This is just one of the reasons why there should be a change in the paradigm of care. The benefits of Telehealth, which is health services delivered eletronically and at distance, go from better patient engagement and satisfaction to cost reduction for both hospitals and patients.

With that in mind, let's dive into an experiment to find out what the ideal digital platform would be like in terms of usability, disaribility, and accessibility.

EMPATHISING

The Context

From the patient perspective, because that's the audience we want to better serve. There will be a more notable impact if the healthcare system is improved for the patients.

When:

The interaction with the system will probably start with users feeling sick and trying to get an appointment or get diagnosed from where they are.

Where:

Patients could be anywhere. At home, school, work, traveling, etc.

Feelings:

Users probably feel anxious and uncertain.

Most popular reasons for patients to visit a clinic: prescription renewal, general checkup, blood pressure disorder, sick note for employer, back-pain, mental health, diabetes, vaccination, etc.

Current Customer Journey

Simplified and from the patients' point of view

Pain-Points

- Risk of contracting other diseases from the hospital;

- Risk of forgetting insurance card behind;

- Symptoms search and selection;

- Envolving family or friends in the process.

- Physical effort when users are feeling sick;

IDEATION

Possible Solutions

1. Virtual

So users can have a first contact and quickly diagnosed without a trip to the hospital.

2. Communication

So users can get clarification on quick questions about their condition or general health.

3. Healthcare Marketplace

So families can find clinics that have partnership with their insucarance company as well as family doctors.

4. e-Health Patient Portal / e-Health Doctor Portal

So patients can have easy access to their health history. And so doctors can easily exchange information with fellow colleagues.

Essential Features

- Insurance and payment collection;

- Live consultation;

- Symptoms search and selection;

- Photo, video and file sharing;

- Patient health history track;

- Quick Access to Exam Results

Main Focus Goals

Prioritizing

Which one of these ideas is going to bring more value for users and a potential increase in revenue for hospitals?

Let's compare the 4 ideas to understand which of them will bring more significant ans positive impact to Healthcare taking into account the amount of effort needed.

The idea that offers more potential for positive impact in the healthcare system, both for patients and clinics workforce, and at the same time is well aligned with business goals (reducing costs and optimizing time) is to build a Virtual Consultation Platform.
