- About me

"From Account management to leading classrooms, I've always been committed to solving people's problems to get them closer to their goals."

Have you always worked with Product Design/UX?

In my previous role I worked as an English Teacher, which I consider a highlight in my career. I believe that if one can overcome all the challenges that teaching bears and create A-HA moments in other people's lives, there's nothing one can't do. It was really rewarding to teach people from all different backgrounds, ages and countries. I value experiences that bring me closer to the rest of the world.

I have also worked in Customer Business Management and Financial Controlling. From elaborating business cases and economic feasibilities, and conducting monthly financial meetings, the core of my roles has always been based on understanding customer's problems thoroughly and working out solutions.

How did you get into UX Design?

In 2015, as the final assignemnt of my Business Management course in UC Berkeley, I did a Business Case for a Home Services App. The goal of the app was to create a tool for matching home services workers with families in need of a painter, for instance. Anything from Home Maintenance to Repair and Beauty services. While studying the functional and visual aspects of the app, I stumbled upon UX and, not to sound cliché here, but I fell in love with it. I never stopped self-learning since then.

In 2019, I decided it was time to take a step further into my studies and get certified in UX Design. In April 2020, I acquired my Professional Diploma in UX Design from UX Design Institute.

What skills have you acquired since you started studying at UX Design Institute?

From empathising to prototyping, completing the course provided me with real-world experience in UX. I feel confident in conducting user research and usability tests, building affinity diagrams, customer journey maps and creating wireframes and prototypes. I've also become more comfortable using tools such as Figma, Sketch and Adobe XD.

What have you been doing to continue improving your skills in UX/UI Design?

I've been practicing the UX Process through Case Studies. I started the UI Daily Challenge to practice and experiment in this field as well. As part of my practice, I read a lot to keep my projects well aligned with UX/UI trends. I'm also working hard to master as many tools as I can. Mostly, I've been working with Figma, Adobe XD and Adobe's Creative Suite.
